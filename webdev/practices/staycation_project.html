Humble Beginnings

The start of this band begins with the end of one. In the late summer of 1995, a band named Kyuss broke up after 8 years of playing together. Guitarist Josh Hommes figured that he was about done with the music industry. He considered studying business at the University of Washington, but he was convinced to stay in the music industry after he was offered to be a touring guitarist for a band named Screaming Trees. While on the tour, Josh met Mark Lanegan, who ended up being the aiding vocalist in QOTSA. With former members of Kyuss (Bassist Nick Oliveri and drummer Alfredo Hernandez), Josh realized he could start a band. Josh ended up being the guitarist and lead singer for the band. He named his band Queens of the Stone Age because he wanted to escape the restrictive "stoner rock" label and create a sound he described as "trance robot music for girls"—heavy enough for guys but melodic and groovy enough to dance to.

Queens of the Stone Age's self-titled debut album was released on September 22, 1998, via Loosegroove Records. Recorded in Palm Desert and Joshua Tree, California, it was mostly produced by Josh Homme and Chris Goss, featuring Homme on vocals and guitar and Alfredo Hernández on drums. It is known for its raw, "desert rock" sound. In 2002, the band released their most famous album, "Songs for the Deaf". This album featured Dave Grohl on the drums, known for drumming for Nirvana and starting the band Foo Fighters. This helped them gain the fame that they desired. Even though Dave Grohl only drummed for that album, it gave QOTSA the social boost that they needed. This collaboration resulted in hits like 'No One Knows,' which dominated alternative radio and introduced a wider audience to Homme’s signature stoner-rock riffs. Today, the album is often cited as one of the greatest hard rock records of the 21st century.
